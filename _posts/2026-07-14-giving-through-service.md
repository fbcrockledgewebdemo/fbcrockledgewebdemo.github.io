Giving is often associated with money, but Scripture presents a much broader picture of generosity. God has entrusted each of us with time, strength, abilities, resources, and opportunities to care for others. Service is one of the clearest ways we can offer those gifts back to Him.

Jesus consistently demonstrated this kind of giving. He welcomed those who were overlooked, cared for the suffering, fed the hungry, and placed the needs of others before His own comfort. His life reminds us that genuine service begins with love and humility.

Serving others does not always require a large project or public recognition. It may mean preparing a meal for a grieving family, visiting someone who is lonely, helping a neighbor with a difficult task, volunteering in a church ministry, or simply taking time to listen to someone who needs encouragement. Small acts of faithfulness can carry great meaning when they are offered with compassion.

Christian service also calls us to notice needs beyond our immediate circles. Communities are filled with people facing hunger, illness, financial hardship, isolation, and uncertainty. The church has an opportunity to meet these needs while sharing the hope found in Jesus Christ. A gift, a meal, or a helping hand can open the door to prayer, encouragement, and the message of the Gospel.

Our attitude matters as much as the service itself. We do not serve to appear generous or to receive praise. We serve because Christ first loved us. Our actions become an expression of gratitude for His grace and a visible testimony of His character.

Every believer has something to offer. Some can give their labor. Others can teach, organize, cook, repair, encourage, pray, or provide transportation. God can use every willing servant and every faithful contribution.

As we consider how to give, we should ask where God has placed us and whose needs are already within our reach. Giving through service begins with a willing heart, open eyes, and the simple decision to act.

> “As each one has received a gift, minister it to one another, as good stewards of the manifold grace of God.”
> — 1 Peter 4:10

May we use what God has given us to strengthen the church, care for our neighbors, and make the love of Christ known through both our words and our actions.
